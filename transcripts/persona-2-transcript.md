# Session Transcript: Persona 2 (The Victim)

**Character Profile:** Low agency, focus on entitlement, narrow radius of concern.

---

### Axis 1: Locus (Victim ↔ Victor)
- **Question:** If you had to describe the 'weather' of your workday today, what would it be?
- **Answer:** **Stormy (Challenging)**
- **System Logic:** External Locus path activated.
- **Question:** Today felt Stormy (Challenging). When the 'weather' turned, where did your focus go first?
- **Answer:** **To the external factors causing the friction**
- **Question:** In that moment of friction, did you feel like a participant or a spectator?
- **Answer:** **A target of the circumstances**
- **Reflection:** "Agency isn't about controlling the weather; it's about how you pilot the ship. You noticed 'target' as your primary mode. That's your locus of control in action."

### Axis 2: Orientation (Contribution ↔ Entitlement)
- **Question:** Think of your energy today as a currency. How did you spend most of it?
- **Answer:** **Protecting myself from interruptions/demands**
- **Question:** Was there a moment today where you felt you deserved more than what you received (credit, time, help)?
- **Answer:** **Yes, I felt my contribution wasn't fully recognized**
- **Question:** When you think about your team's success today, how much of it felt like 'your' win versus 'their' responsibility?
- **Answer:** **I felt they owed me more support**
- **Reflection:** "It's natural to feel like the scale is unbalanced sometimes. Entitlement is often just a shield for feeling undervalued. Recognizing it is the first step toward reclaiming your power to give."

### Axis 3: Radius (Self-Centrism ↔ Altrocentrism)
- **Question:** When you encountered a challenge today, who was in the frame of your thoughts?
- **Answer:** **Mostly me—how it affected my stress and my goals**
- **Question:** If you could change one person's experience of today (besides your own), who would it be?
- **Answer:** **I didn't really think about others' experiences today**
- **Question:** When you think about the impact of your work today, where does the ripple end?
- **Answer:** **With my own completion of tasks**
- **Reflection:** "A narrow radius is often a survival mechanism for a high-stress day. But even a small shift outward—noticing a teammate's effort—can break the cycle of self-centered stress."

### Final Summary
- **Text:** "Reflection Complete. Today, you navigated with **external** agency, showed a **entitlement** orientation, and maintained a **self** radius of concern."
- **Closing:** "Rest well. Tomorrow is a new day to choose how you show up."
